# Semester 2 Project - RLA

> A small to medium‑sized **medical delivery company** operates daily in an urban and peri‑urban environment. Its core activity consists of delivering **medical goods** (such as medicines or medical supplies) to pharmacies, clinics, and  healthcare facilities using a limited fleet of vehicles and a small team of delivery drivers.
>
> As the company grows, the organization of deliveries becomes  increasingly complex. Each day, the company must handle multiple  delivery addresses, allocate deliveries to different drivers, and decide the order in which deliveries should be performed. These decisions  directly impact **delivery reliability, public safety, operational costs, customer satisfaction, and driver workload**.
>
> A critical constraint of the business is that **medical products cannot remain in a transport vehicle for more than 24 hours**. Deliveries must therefore be planned carefully to respect this temporal limitation, while still accounting for operational realities.
